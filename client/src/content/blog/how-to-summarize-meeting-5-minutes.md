Most meeting summaries take 20–30 minutes to write. They also don't get read. Here's how to do it in 5 minutes and make something people actually use.

## The 5-minute process

### Minute 1: Capture raw notes during the meeting

During the meeting, write only:
- Decisions made (mark with D:)
- Action items (mark with A:, include name and deadline)
- Key information that changes someone's work

Don't try to capture everything that was said. If something wasn't a decision or an action, it probably doesn't need to be in the summary.

### Minutes 2–3: Structure the raw notes

Immediately after the meeting (while context is fresh), group your raw notes into:

**Summary sentence:** One sentence — what was this meeting about and what was the main outcome?

**Decisions:** Convert "D:" notes into clear decision statements

**Actions:** Convert "A:" notes into the table format: what / who / by when

### Minute 4: Add recipients and context

Who needs this summary?
- Everyone in the meeting (confirmation of their commitments)
- Anyone who should have been there but wasn't
- Anyone whose work is affected by a decision

Don't copy in the whole company. Copy in the people who need to know.

### Minute 5: Send it

Don't wait until you've polished it. A decent summary sent in 5 minutes is worth 10x more than a perfect one sent 2 days later.

Subject line: "[Project/Team] — Meeting summary [Date]"

## What a good meeting summary looks like

---

**Meeting:** Q2 launch planning — Marketing + Product
**Date:** June 10, 2026
**Attendees:** Emeka, Tunde, Fatima, Bibi

**Summary:** Agreed to push launch from June 30 to July 14. Core reason: payment integration needs 2 more weeks.

**Decisions:**
- Launch date: July 14, 2026
- Marketing campaign to start July 7 (one week before launch)
- Pricing: ₦4,000/month starter plan confirmed (Emeka to confirm with CEO by Friday)

**Actions:**

| What | Who | By |
|---|---|---|
| Complete Squad webhook integration | Tunde | June 17 |
| Draft launch email sequence | Fatima | June 20 |
| Update website copy with new pricing | Bibi | June 21 |
| Get CEO sign-off on pricing | Emeka | June 13 |

**Next meeting:** June 17, 10am — progress check

---

That summary took 5 minutes to write and tells every stakeholder exactly what they need to know.

## Common mistakes that waste time

**Mistake 1: Starting from scratch in a new document**
Keep a running notes file or use the same template every time. Starting fresh adds 5 minutes.

**Mistake 2: Writing full sentences for everything**
Bullets and tables are faster to write and faster to read.

**Mistake 3: Including context that's already documented elsewhere**
If the background is in the project brief, link to it. Don't re-explain it.

**Mistake 4: Waiting for perfect notes**
"I'll clean this up later" is how meeting notes become never-sent notes. Send the rough version now.
