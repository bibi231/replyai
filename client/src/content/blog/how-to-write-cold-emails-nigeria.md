Cold email in Nigeria isn't dead — but the approach that works in San Francisco doesn't translate directly to Lagos or Abuja. Here's what actually works.

## Why generic cold email fails in Nigeria

1. **Decision makers are overloaded.** A Lagos business owner receives dozens of cold messages daily across email, WhatsApp, LinkedIn, and phone. Generic "I noticed your company" emails get deleted immediately.

2. **Trust is built differently.** Nigerian business culture is high-context — relationships and referrals matter more than credentials. A cold email from a stranger needs to work harder to establish credibility.

3. **The ask is often too big too fast.** "Let's schedule a 30-minute call" is a significant request from someone you've never interacted with. Start smaller.

## The cold email formula that works

### Subject line (the most important 6 words)

Your subject line determines whether the email is opened. Effective patterns:

- Specific problem: "Your checkout page — I found something"
- Mutual connection: "[Name] suggested I reach out"
- Specific result: "Doubled [similar company]'s leads in 6 weeks"
- Curiosity + specificity: "Question about your [specific thing]"

Avoid: "Partnership opportunity", "Quick question" (overused), "Following up" (for a cold email you've never sent)

### Opening line (don't compliment)

Don't start with "I love what you're doing with [Company]" — it reads as flattery. Start with something specific and useful.

Good openers:
- "I found a broken link on your [specific page] — might be costing you leads"
- "I saw your team is hiring 3 engineers — that usually means a scaling challenge"
- "Your [competitor] just started doing [thing] — worth knowing if you haven't seen it"

### The ask (smaller than you think)

Nigerian decision makers are skeptical of large time commitments from strangers. Your ask should be tiny:

- Reply with yes/no
- A single question for them to answer
- 10 minutes (not 30)
- Forward to the right person if they're not the decision maker

### The close

Brief, specific, no-pressure:
"If this isn't relevant, no worries at all — I know your time is valuable. But if [specific outcome] is something you're working on, I'd love to share how we've approached it."

## The full template

Subject: [Specific thing you noticed about their business]

Hi [First Name],

[Specific observation about their business or industry — one sentence that shows you actually looked]

[What you do + one specific result you've achieved for a similar company — two sentences max]

[Small, specific ask — one sentence]

[No-pressure close]

[Your name, title, company]

## Follow-up after no response

Wait 4–5 business days. One follow-up is appropriate. Two is the limit.

Follow-up format: "Just wanted to bump this in case it got buried. Happy to take it in a different direction if [my original ask] isn't the right fit."

Never guilt-trip, never get passive-aggressive, never send more than 2 follow-ups.

## What not to do

- Don't send from a Gmail address for business cold email — it signals you're not serious
- Don't attach a deck to the first email — it adds friction and looks like spam
- Don't use bulk email tools with merge tags that fail ("Hi {FIRST_NAME}")
- Don't send Monday morning (high competition for inbox attention)

Best days: Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday. Best time: 8am or 1pm WAT.
