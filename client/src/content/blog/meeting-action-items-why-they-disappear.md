Studies consistently show that 40–60% of meeting action items are never completed. This isn't because people are lazy — it's because the system for capturing and tracking them is broken.

## Why action items disappear

### The vagueness problem

"Follow up on the vendor" is not an action item. It's a vague intention. Action items that disappear are usually:
- Not assigned to a specific person
- Missing a specific deadline
- Not specific enough about what "done" looks like

### The burial problem

Action items written in the middle of meeting notes — between paragraphs of context and discussion — don't get seen. The person responsible for an action has to search for their name in the notes.

### The distribution problem

Meeting notes sent to everyone as a document or email require each person to find their own tasks. Most people don't. The people who do are the ones who would have done the task anyway.

### The accountability gap

When action items aren't tracked, there's no moment of accountability. The next meeting starts, nobody asks "did you complete X?", and the item quietly dies.

## The system that works

### 1. The action item table (mandatory format)

Every meeting ends with a table. No exceptions:

| Action | Owner | Due | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| Draft RFP for vendor selection | Chidi | June 14 | Pending |
| Confirm budget approval | Amaka | June 12 | Pending |
| Update project timeline | PM | June 13 | Pending |

Four columns: what, who, due date, status (updated at next meeting).

### 2. Direct assignment notifications

Don't just send the full meeting summary to the group. Also send individual messages to each person with their specific action items:

"Hi [Name] — from today's meeting, you have: [specific task] by [date]. Let me know if anything is unclear."

On WhatsApp, this takes 60 seconds and increases completion rates dramatically.

### 3. The 48-hour check-in

Two days after the meeting, a brief check-in: "Quick check — is [task] on track for [date]?"

This isn't micromanagement — it's a reminder before the deadline, not after. People appreciate it.

### 4. The standing review at the start of next meeting

Every meeting that follows another meeting starts with 5 minutes on action items from last time. Not to shame anyone, but to maintain the accountability loop.

Format: "From last time — [person], where are we on [task]?"
